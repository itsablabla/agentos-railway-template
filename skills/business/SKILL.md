# Business Skill

Strategic frameworks and tools for business analysis, planning, and communication.

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when developing business strategy, creating financial models or projections,
writing executive communications, evaluating business opportunities, or preparing board materials.

## Strategy Frameworks

### Porter's Five Forces
Analyze industry competitiveness across five dimensions: threat of new entrants,
bargaining power of suppliers, bargaining power of buyers, threat of substitutes,
and competitive rivalry.

### Business Model Canvas
Nine building blocks: Value Propositions, Customer Segments, Channels,
Customer Relationships, Revenue Streams, Key Resources, Key Activities,
Key Partnerships, and Cost Structure.

### Unit Economics
Key SaaS and subscription metrics:
- CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) = Sales & Marketing Spend / New Customers
- LTV (Lifetime Value) = ARPU × Gross Margin × Average Customer Lifetime
- LTV:CAC Ratio = LTV / CAC (target: greater than 3x)
- Payback Period = CAC / (ARPU × Gross Margin) (target: less than 12 months)

### OKR Framework
Each Objective is a qualitative, inspiring goal supported by 3 Key Results that are
measurable outcomes (not activities). Key Results should be specific, time-bound, and verifiable.

## Executive Communication Templates

### Board Update (1-pager)
Headline with company name and month. Performance vs. plan section showing revenue and
key metrics with variance percentages. Top 3 wins with impact. Top 3 risks with mitigations.
Specific asks from the board.

### Investment Memo Structure
1. Executive Summary (1 page)
2. Problem and Solution
3. Market Size (TAM/SAM/SOM)
4. Business Model and Unit Economics
5. Traction and Milestones
6. Team
7. Financials and Use of Funds
8. Risks and Mitigations

## Financial Modeling Standards
Use consistent time periods (monthly for under 2 years, annual after).
Separate assumptions from calculations. Include sensitivity analysis for key drivers.
Show three scenarios: base, bull, and bear. Always verify the math adds up.
